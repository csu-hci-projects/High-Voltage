Introduction

This project will undertake the challenge of measuring the efficacy of using AR as a method of delivering training that fulfills the need for hands on training while conveying the experience to the technical student in a meaningful and memorable way. Additionally, I contend that this experience will provide an increased level of retention over the more traditional remote learning format of recorded videos.

Methodology

By utilizing AR technology, an AWA-IV, Advanced Winding Analyzer seen in figure 1, will be mapped to the test device and provide points of interaction will be overlaid on some of the analyzers primary interface buttons and dials, concept image shown in figure 2. When the subject interacts with the highlighted areas on the screen the application will begin describing the proper operation and use of the control feature selected. A video containing equivalent content will also be produced as a training aid for comparison purposes by experiment.

Results

Users felt that the AR experience was exciting and caused them to engage more with the training activity than previous video experiences had. Users all tested at or above 80% on the delivered content. Almost all users expressed frustration with the clickable interface. Onscreen click detection was not the most sensitive and required several presses to initiate the audio portion.

Discussion

From this study we have learned that the AR experience is not any more effective than traditional methods. It does however engage the students with a new excitement that they have not experienced before.Future work will use this feedback and create a novel experience that draws in users and improves the user experience so that it is seamless and intuitive.
